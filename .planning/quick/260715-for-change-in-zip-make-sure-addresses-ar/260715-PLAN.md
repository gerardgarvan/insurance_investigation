<objective>
Tighten R/106_zip_change_frequency.R's Section 9 ("Time Between Changes" / Sheet 3)
so that ADDRESS_PERIOD_START dates outside the actual LDS_ADDRESS_HISTORY study
period (January 1, 2012 - March 31, 2025) are excluded from the gap-day
computation, instead of silently polluting the median/p25/p75 gap stats and the
Sheet 5 recommendation text. Currently Section 9 only checks `!is.na()` on the
parsed date -- it has no sanity bound at all, so SAS/Excel epoch sentinels or
dates past the data-collection cutoff would flow straight into
`difftime()`-based gap-day math.

Purpose: Prevent garbage/out-of-range dates from corrupting the ZIP-change-gap
statistics that Phase 121's SES-index recommendation is based on.
Output: Two new named date-bound constants in R/106, a tightened Section 9
filter with a logged drop-count, and matching R/88 Section 15s structural
checks that verify the fix is present.
</objective>

             period_start_dt <= ZIP_STUDY_PERIOD_MAX,
             !is.na(zip9_norm)
